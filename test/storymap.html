The Tea Horse Road

For centuries, brave traders traversed the treacherous paths of the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau. They traded Chinese tea for Tibetan horses, forging a network of paths that linked cultures, religions, and people.

Scroll down to trace their journey.

Dali: Wind, Flowers, Snow, Moon

Nestled between the Cangshan Mountains and Erhai Lake. Dali was the ancient capital of the Nanzhao and Dali Kingdoms.

Here, life slows down. The Bai people worship their white architecture and the eternal spring.

Lijiang: Naxi Heritage

Further north lies Lijiang, a maze of cobblestone streets and canals. This is the heart of the Naxi culture.

Dominated by the Jade Dragon Snow Mountain, it is a place where music and Dongba hieroglyphs survive.

Shangri-La: The Lost Horizon

At 3,160 meters, the air thins. You have arrived in Gyalthang (Shangri-La). This is the gateway to Tibet.

Golden monasteries gleam against the backdrop of snowy peaks. It is savage, holy, and breathtaking.
